Ada Lovelace

World's First Programmer

Here is a time line of Ada Lovelace's life:

  • -1815: Born to Lord Byron as his only legitimate child as the Countess of Lovelace
  • -1824: Lord Byron dies when she is 8 years old
  • -1829: Ada was paralyzed from a bout of measles confined to bederest
  • -1831: She was able to walk crutches after being bedridden while developing her mathematical and technological skills
  • -1833: Ada is introduced to Charles Babage through her tutor Mary Somerville
  • -1835: Married to William King-Noel thus becoming the Lady King
  • -1843: Lovelace translated a article and added notes based on the Analytical Machine which was well received by the scientific community. These notes describe an algorithm which is now considered the world's first.
  • -1851: She attempted with friends to devise a mathematical means for successfully large bets
  • -1852: Lovelace died the same age as her father from uterine cancer. She was buried in Nottinghamshire
